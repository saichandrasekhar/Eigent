Compliance & EU AI Act

Eigent's architecture maps directly to regulatory requirements. Every delegation, every tool call, every denial is compliance evidence by default.

EU AI Act Article Mapping

The EU AI Act (effective August 2025) requires risk management, logging, transparency, human oversight, and quality management for high-risk AI systems. Eigent's five primitives directly satisfy these requirements.

ARTICLE 9 — Risk Management

Risk Management System

Requires identification and mitigation of risks from AI systems throughout their lifecycle.

Eigent: Scope intersection limits what agents can do. Permission narrowing ensures sub-agents have fewer capabilities. Cascade revocation provides immediate risk mitigation.
ARTICLE 12 — Record-Keeping

Automatic Logging

Requires automatic logging of events during AI system operation, with traceability throughout the lifecycle.

Eigent: Merkle-chained audit trail logs every token issuance, delegation, tool call, allow, and deny. Tamper-evident by construction. Every entry includes the full delegation chain.
ARTICLE 13 — Transparency

Transparency & Information

Requires that AI systems are sufficiently transparent to enable users to interpret and use output appropriately.

Eigent: The delegation chain in every token makes the provenance of every action transparent. Denial responses include full chain context and reason codes.
ARTICLE 14 — Human Oversight

Human Oversight Measures

Requires that AI systems can be effectively overseen by natural persons, including the ability to intervene or interrupt.

Eigent: Every agent chain traces back to a human. Humans can revoke any agent at any time with cascade revocation. Scope limits prevent agents from exceeding human intent.
ARTICLE 17 — Quality Management

Quality Management System

Requires systematic management of AI system quality, including risk management procedures and reporting.

Eigent: The registry maintains a complete inventory of all agents, their permissions, chains, and status. Compliance reports can be generated on demand.

What the Audit Trail Proves

During a compliance review or incident investigation, the Eigent audit trail answers these questions definitively:

ACCOUNTABILITY

"Which human authorized this agent to perform this action?"

PROVENANCE

"What was the complete chain of delegations that led to this action?"

SCOPE

"Was this agent authorized to perform this specific action at this time?"

PREVENTION

"What unauthorized actions were blocked, and why?"

INTEGRITY

"Has this audit log been tampered with?" (Merkle chain verification)

REVOCATION

"When was this agent's access revoked, and what was the impact?"
